Use this skill when the Research Task needs candidate hypotheses, limitation
analysis, route selection, or a new Research Branch from current evidence.

Read first:

- `../isomer-labs-research-shared/SKILL.md`
- Source analysis: `../../../context/explore/deepscientist-skill-analysis/idea.md`

## Entry Signals

- The Research Task needs hypotheses, limitation analysis, route selection, or
  a new Research Branch.
- The current evidence board is sufficiently reconciled to compare candidate
  routes.
- False-progress signals, hard constraints, or abandonment conditions need to
  be made explicit before experimentation.

## Exit Criteria

- Objective contract, current-board packet, and candidate frontier are durable.
- One falsifiable route is selected, or the blocker preventing selection is
  recorded.
- The route handoff names the next stage: optimize, experiment, scout,
  baseline, decision, or blocker.

## Procedure

1. Write an objective contract: target, trusted proxies, false-progress
   signals, hard constraints, and abandonment conditions.
2. Build a current-board packet with incumbent evidence, decisive results,
   blockers, and stale routes.
3. Identify the bottleneck, anomaly, or gap that should drive ideation.
4. Check relevant prior work and local Findings before serious proposals.
5. Separate candidate families by mechanism, objective, measurement, and
   infrastructure.
6. Draft a small differentiated slate, then challenge serious candidates.
7. Select one falsifiable route or record why the slate is not ready.

## Durable Outputs

- Objective contract Artifact.
- Current-board packet Artifact.
- Candidate frontier with challenge notes.
- Selected route Artifact and Decision Record, or blocker.

## Guardrails

- Do not brainstorm before false-progress signals are explicit.
- Do not promote decorative tweaks as novelty.
- Do not skip prior-work and history checks before route selection.
- Use `[[tbd-surface:policy-branching]]` when branch promotion is unsettled.
