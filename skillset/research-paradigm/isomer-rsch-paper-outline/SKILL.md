Use this skill when a report or paper needs a valid outline before drafting or
when an existing outline does not match the evidence.

Read first:

- `../isomer-rsch-shared/SKILL.md`
- Source analysis: `../../../context/explore/deepscientist-skill-analysis/paper-outline.md`

## Entry Signals

- A report or paper needs an outline before drafting.
- An existing outline does not match the evidence, claims, method abstraction,
  evaluation plan, or reviewer-facing analysis plan.
- The paper idea must be separated from raw run logs and unsupported claims.

## Exit Criteria

- Paper view, evidence view, and section-level writing plan are durable.
- Scoped claims have evidence, boundaries, and falsification conditions.
- Unsupported claims and missing evidence are visible before drafting.

## Procedure

1. Inspect current outline, paper contract, evidence ledger, and report state.
2. Write the one-sentence reader-facing idea.
3. Separate measured facts from interpretation and claims.
4. Build the paper view: thesis, story spine, scoped claims, method
   abstraction, evaluation plan, and analysis plan.
5. Build the evidence view: runs, settings, metrics, sources, caveats, and
   reproducibility details.
6. Validate claim support, falsification boundaries, and reviewer-facing
   analysis plan.
7. Repair the outline until it can compile into section writing jobs.

## Durable Outputs

- Candidate, selected, or revised outline Artifact.
- Paper view and evidence view.
- Section-level writing plan.

## Guardrails

- Do not copy run logs into the manuscript plan.
- Do not treat a section list as a mature outline.
- Every claim needs evidence and boundary conditions.
- Use `[[tbd-surface:api-artifact-record]]` for unsettled outline recording.
