# Evidence Authenticity Checklist

Use this checklist before calling a draft strong, final, or submission-ready.

## Inventory

Build an inventory from durable Evidence Items, result files, evaluation summaries, run records, tables, figures, captions, claim-evidence maps, and manuscript claims. For each main experiment and analysis item, record expected purpose, status, metrics actually present, current or stale state, manuscript placement, supported claim, and caveat.

## Classification

Classify every important result as one of: completed and written, completed but not written, written but not evidenced, appendix-only, failed or negative, stale or duplicate, superseded, contradicted, or blocked. Negative and failed results remain evidence and should stay visible.

## Fabrication and Overclaim Risk

Assess each serious claim with these labels: no issue found, provenance gap, manuscript overclaim, written result unsupported by durable evidence, contradiction between manuscript and artifacts, or likely false claim. If any material claim falls outside evidence, route to write for claim narrowing or to analysis for real follow-up evidence.

## Coverage Count

Do not trust ready counts when duplicate rows, stale outline references, pending rows, missing metrics, or appendix-only rows inflate the package. Recompute the paper-facing experiment or analysis group count manually against the current claim set.

## Submission-Ready Floor

A paper cannot be called submission-ready unless manuscript coverage, evidence provenance, citation sufficiency, language hygiene, figure and table status, rendered bundle or build state, experiment-matrix consistency, and unresolved Gates all pass.

## Review Questions

Can the reviewer inspect the main claim from main text? Do figures and tables answer concrete questions? Are citations real and sufficient? Does the manuscript leak process language? Are existing results unmapped? Are missing experiments real evidence gaps or only presentation gaps?
