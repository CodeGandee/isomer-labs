# Literature Benchmark

Use this reference when a review must judge novelty, paper quality, venue fit, citation sufficiency, or experiment-package maturity against nearby work.

## When to Run

Run a literature benchmark before calling a substantial manuscript strong unless the user explicitly limits the review to local evidence. Use Literature Provider Binding refs for provider-backed search and record context-only results as provider-output Artifacts before deriving Findings or Evidence Items.

## Comparison Set

Build a compact comparison set, usually three to eight papers: one to three closest technical neighbors, one to three strong writing or story exemplars from high-quality venues, and one to two experiment-package exemplars when evaluation design is uncertain.

## What to Record

For each comparator, record title, venue or source, why it is relevant, trust level, and what it teaches about abstract and introduction framing, problem-to-gap-to-method logic, method explanation, experiment design, ablations, robustness, baselines, limitations, display roles, related-work positioning, and claim boundaries.

## Route Discipline

Do not request new experiments just because literature positioning is weak. First decide whether the fix is writing, related-work repair, claim narrowing, baseline recovery, or true evidence generation.

## Output Shape

The review report should include a comparison matrix when literature standards affect the judgment. Keep it compact and grounded. Do not fabricate venues, acceptance status, or paper details.
