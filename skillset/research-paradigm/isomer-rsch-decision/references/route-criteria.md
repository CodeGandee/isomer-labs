# Route Criteria

Use this reference when choosing among Research Inquiry Relationships, idea packages, experiment groups, analysis campaigns, paper-facing routes, or blockers.

## Core Question

What exact insufficiency are we trying to resolve now?

Do not choose a route because it sounds sophisticated or creates activity. Choose the smallest route that can change the Research Claim, unblock the next stage, or prevent wasted work.

## Preferred Route Profile

Prefer routes that attack a real bottleneck, stay compatible with the existing architecture when possible, are implementable with bounded risk, can produce interpretable Evidence Items, preserve comparability, and can be defended in later writing, review, or rebuttal.

## Tradeoffs to Name

- novelty versus feasibility
- expected gain versus verification cost
- architectural fit versus rewrite burden
- immediate progress versus long-term research value
- elegant mechanism versus brute-force scaling
- publishing value versus evidence sufficiency
- reopening an old route versus protecting a current checkpoint

## Selection Output

A good route decision records the winner, strongest alternatives, criteria, expected learning, why rejected options lost, residual risk, and exact next Workflow Stage.

## Negative-Result Paper Gate

Before recommending a write route for an all-negative-result paper, confirm whether the work tested structurally distinct idea families, challenged the bottleneck framing, checked literature for different routes, and used the available budget responsibly. If those answers are no or unsure and time remains, route back to ideation or decision instead of prematurely closing the design space.
