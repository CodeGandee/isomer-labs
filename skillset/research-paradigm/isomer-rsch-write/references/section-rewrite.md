# Section Rewrite Checklist

Use this checklist when upgrading or repairing specific manuscript sections.

## Abstract

Check that the abstract moves from problem or deficiency to proposed idea, difference or principle, main empirical pattern, strongest concrete outcomes, and calibrated takeaway. Rewrite overloaded number dumps, lab-note summaries, missing one-sentence memory, and strict dominance claims that the evidence cannot support.

## Introduction

Check that the introduction states the problem, explains why current approaches are insufficient, introduces the core idea in plain language, provides an early intuition anchor, identifies the operating regime, places the paper in its broader method family, makes novelty legible, and ends with clear contributions or roadmap.

## Related Work

Check that related work names closest neighbors, separates similar goal from similar mechanism, and defines the novelty boundary. Rewrite bibliography dumps and late positioning that leave reviewers unsure what is new.

## Method

Check that the method defines the method precisely, gives intuition, explains why it should work, compares it to alternatives, and tells the reader what property to watch for in experiments. Keep implementation statements anchored to verified code, configuration, scripts, or outputs.

## Experiments

Check that experiments introduce setup clearly, state the main pattern before numbers, use displays that match claims, separate main results from support, anticipate objections, and give each display one dominant local claim.

## Analysis

Check that analysis explains mechanism, addresses reviewer concerns, connects back to claims, names stable trends, and uses prose to explain why patterns appear. Rewrite analysis that repeats tables or feels like leftover supplementary material.

## Figures and Tables

For each display, ask why it appears, what question it answers, whether the caption is independently readable, whether surrounding prose states the takeaway, whether it belongs in main text or appendix, and whether the display type matches its job.

## Conclusion, Limitations, and Reproducibility

The conclusion should restate the contribution and scope without adding unsupported claims. Limitations should calibrate trust, not check a box. Reproducibility and artifact availability should be consistent across abstract, main text, appendix, and bundle metadata.

## Appendix

The appendix should have explicit jobs: protocol detail, full results, extra ablations, tuned baselines, sensitivity checks, proofs, failure cases, examples, and reproducibility material. Rewrite appendices that look like unstructured leftovers.
