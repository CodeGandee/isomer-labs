# Isomer Research Write

## Overview

Use this skill when a Research Task has enough durable evidence to draft, revise, package, or route a paper, report, checkpoint, review packet, or oral-quality manuscript upgrade.

## Workflow

When this skill is invoked, execute the following steps in order.

1. **Load required context**. Read `references/writing-contract.md` first and read `references/provenance.md` when source provenance or license context matters.
2. **Select supporting references** from **Reference Routing** when citation handling, manuscript cleanliness, oral-quality revision, experiments or analysis writing, section-level rewriting, or deferred venue templates matter.
3. **Confirm entry fit and lock the writing object**. Use **Entry Signals** to confirm the task is writing or paper repair, not new evidence generation, baseline recovery, or review response orchestration.
4. **Refresh the paper contract**. Align outline, claim-evidence map, experiment or analysis matrix, figure and table status, bibliography status, manuscript bundle status, and unresolved Gates.
5. **Plan the writing pass before prose**. Separate evidence gaps, manuscript-mapping gaps, unsupported writing, narrative gaps, citation gaps, and metadata drift, then choose one action per issue.
6. **Draft or revise by bounded section jobs**. Keep each claim inside its Evidence Items, keep implementation details in the right manuscript surface, and route displays through the appropriate figure or plot skill.
7. **Validate and route**. Check claim support, citation truth, display readiness, language cleanliness, bundle readiness, and next-stage routing before treating the writing pass as durable.

If the user's task does not map cleanly to these steps, use your native planning tool to build a step-by-step plan from the constraints, references, and user request, then execute the plan.

## Reference Routing

Read first:

- `references/writing-contract.md` for Isomer writing surfaces, source-term mappings, evidence boundaries, runtime boundaries, and TBD-surface rules.
- `references/provenance.md` when source provenance or license context matters.

Read references as needed:

- `references/citation-and-bibliography.md` when the work touches references, BibTeX, literature support, citation counts, or provider-backed literature output.
- `references/manuscript-cleanliness.md` before putting local process, execution, operator, or provenance details into manuscript prose.
- `references/oral-package.md` when upgrading a dense draft into an oral-quality paper package or comparing paper versions.
- `references/oral-writing.md` when narrative spine, reader onboarding, section jobs, claim calibration, or display prose is weak.
- `references/experiments-analysis.md` when experiments and analysis sections need reviewer-question structure, display roles, or interpretive separation.
- `references/section-rewrite.md` when revising abstract, introduction, related work, method, experiments, analysis, conclusion, limitations, reproducibility, or appendix.
- `references/deferred-venue-templates.md` when the user asks about venue LaTeX templates or bundle assets.

## Entry Signals

- A draft, outline, paper contract, report, or review package exists and needs evidence-bound prose.
- A claim-evidence map, experiment matrix, figure or table catalog, bibliography, or bundle manifest needs synchronization before writing.
- The user asks for manuscript drafting, section revision, report writing, checkpoint packaging, or oral-quality paper upgrade.
- The strongest blocker is communication, structure, citation truth, display integration, or bundle readiness rather than missing evidence.

## Exit Criteria

- The updated manuscript sections, report, checkpoint, review package, or bundle Artifact is durable.
- Each material claim maps to Evidence Items, verified citations, or an explicit gap.
- Bibliography, figures, tables, appendix bridges, and manuscript availability statements are consistent with the evidence inventory.
- Any route-back to paper-outline, paper-plot, figure-polish, review, rebuttal, analysis, scout, baseline, decision, finalize, or blocker is recorded as a Decision Record or Gate.

## Durable Outputs

- Updated paper, report, section, or manuscript package Artifact.
- Aligned claim-evidence map, experiment or analysis matrix, bibliography, figure/table status, appendix bridge map, and bundle status.
- Optional section rewrite plan, oral delta map, main-text versus appendix allocation plan, or writing-only versus evidence-dependent fix list.
- Decision Record for claim narrowing, route-back, blocker, review package, or submission-readiness Gate.

## Guardrails

- Do not write citations, metrics, method details, benchmark facts, implementation claims, or artifact availability from recollection.
- Do not make unsupported claims sound more convincing; obtain evidence, narrow the claim, or mark the blocker.
- Do not present baseline-dependent claims unless the accepted comparator is durable or a Baseline-Waiver Policy ref plus required Gate or Decision Record is durable.
- Do not put operator instructions, route history, local execution shorthand, source runtime wording, or draft scaffolding into manuscript prose.
- Do not treat compile success, section count, display count, or bundle existence as manuscript quality.
- Do not use polishing as a substitute for missing baselines, missing analysis, weak novelty, or collapsed publishability.
- Do not import venue templates or write into a concrete paper layout unless that surface is provided by the user or resolved as a paper Artifact through Workspace Path Resolution.
