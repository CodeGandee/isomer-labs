Use this skill when a draft or report needs independent review against claims,
evidence, novelty, figures, language, and likely rejection paths.

Read first:

- `../isomer-labs-research-shared/SKILL.md`
- Source analysis: `../../../context/explore/deepscientist-skill-analysis/review.md`

## Entry Signals

- A substantial draft, report, or paper-like Artifact needs independent review.
- Claims, evidence, novelty, figures, language, or likely rejection paths need
  skeptical audit.
- The review can route concrete fixes rather than inventing new research work.

## Exit Criteria

- Review Artifact, revision log, and any evidence TODOs are durable.
- Major weaknesses, missing evidence, novelty risks, and likely rejection paths
  are explicit.
- The handoff routes to write, scout, baseline, analysis, decision, or blocker.

## Procedure

1. Define review scope, claim set, evidence map, likely route, and risks.
2. Compare the draft against nearby strong papers, benchmark expectations, or
   venue norms when relevant.
3. Write a skeptical review with strengths, weaknesses, key issues,
   actionable fixes, storyline advice, experiment inventory, novelty risks,
   and comparison notes.
4. Convert serious issues into a revision log.
5. Create follow-up evidence TODOs only when recorded evidence is truly
   insufficient.
6. Route to write, scout, baseline, analysis, decision, or blocker.

## Durable Outputs

- Review Artifact.
- Revision log Artifact.
- Evidence TODO Artifact when needed.
- Decision Record or route recommendation.

## Guardrails

- Do not write "no weaknesses" without likely rejection paths.
- Do not turn manuscript blockers into fake experiments.
- Do not ask for new evidence before checking recorded evidence.
- Use analysis for follow-up evidence work, not review-only execution.
