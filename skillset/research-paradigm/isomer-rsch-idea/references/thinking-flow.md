# Idea Thinking Flow

Use this reference when the idea stage needs an explicit reasoning path from limitation to selected route.

## Flow

1. Start from the limitation, not the method: state what fails, where it fails, why it matters, and what evidence shows it is real.
2. Separate symptom, mechanism, and consequence: identify what is observed, what might cause it, and why it hurts the target metric or Research Claim.
3. Build competing hypotheses early: keep one main hypothesis and two or three competing hypotheses when the space permits.
4. Translate the problem into a lever bucket: data, model, objective, optimization or training dynamics, inference, evaluation protocol, or infrastructure.
5. Search literature with a reason: every paper should answer whether the idea was tried, whether the failure mode was explained, whether an adjacent mechanism transfers, or what strong prior work may dominate the candidate.
6. Convert literature into a mechanism map: record what the paper changes, why it helps, what assumptions must hold, what evidence supports it, and what boundary remains.
7. Prefer fast falsification: define the minimal experiment, failure result, false-positive confounder, and abandonment condition.
8. Translate to reader value: explain what new knowledge a reader would gain and what table, ablation, failure case, or Evidence Item would make the claim believable.
9. Run an anti-self-deception check: identify strongest prior work, fragile assumption, easier comparator tweak, and result that would quickly embarrass the theory.
10. End with one clear research object: one falsifiable claim, code-level plan, minimal experiment, abandonment rule, and relation-to-literature summary.

## Exit Rule

If the output is still a bag of possibilities, the idea stage is not done.
