# Selection Gate and Handoff

Use this reference when choosing the final idea and preparing the handoff to optimize or experiment.

## Value and Feasibility Screen

Score each serious candidate on a compact one-to-five scale for importance, novelty, feasibility, verifiability, paper or report potential, failure value, and breakthrough or boundary-changing potential.

Check FINER-style criteria:

- Feasible with current data, compute, codebase, and schedule.
- Interesting enough that the target reader or operator would care.
- Novel in a meaningful sense relative to strongest nearby work.
- Ethically acceptable and within policy and privacy boundaries.
- Relevant to an important bottleneck rather than a decorative tweak.

## Lightweight Quality Gate

Score the final serious candidate from zero to two on novelty, falsifiability, feasibility, evidence quality, and constraint fit. If the total is below seven out of ten, do not promote the idea yet.

Hard gates before promotion:

- Literature survey covers at least five and usually five to ten related usable papers, or the shortage is documented.
- Strongest direct-field overlap and adjacent mechanisms are compared.
- A pre-idea draft or equivalent challenge memo exists for the likely winner.
- Hidden assumptions, local-optimum lock-in risk, strongest rejection case, and strongest outside-family alternative are explicit.
- The closest-prior-work comparison explains why the idea is still needed.
- The selected route is labeled honestly as `novel`, `incremental but valuable`, or `not sufficiently differentiated`.

## Handoff Fields

- stable route id
- motivation in situation, complication, question, answer form when useful
- reasoning with main hypothesis and two or three competing hypotheses near the top
- falsifiable claim tied to metric key, expected direction, and boundary condition
- theory and method
- code-level plan
- relation to literature
- citation or bibliography pointers from the survey
- metric key, expected direction, minimal experiment, abandonment condition, and strongest alternative hypothesis
- next Workflow Stage and any Gate or Decision Record needed before execution

## Promotion Rule

Promote only routes labeled `novel` or `incremental but valuable`. Reject or defer routes that are not sufficiently differentiated, lack falsification, lack citation support, or win only because they are familiar or easy to implement.
