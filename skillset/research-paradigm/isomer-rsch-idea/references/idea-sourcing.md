# High-Value Idea Sourcing

Use this reference when the idea pass risks starting from a clever technique rather than an important contradiction. The goal is a route that is worth doing and realistically testable, not a module swap.

## Start from the Important Contradiction

Before asking what mechanism to add, ask what core problem remains unresolved, why existing methods still fail, and which observation, conflict, or failure case the mainline does not explain.

Prefer these sources:

- Unexplained phenomenon: a stylized fact, anomaly, or recurring pattern the current explanation does not fit.
- Failure region: a task, subgroup, regime, or deployment condition where the mainline breaks.
- Evaluation distortion: benchmark scores look strong while stronger diagnostics disagree.
- Theoretical rupture: the method works empirically, but the mechanism or theory remains weak.
- Neglected problem slice: an important subgroup, setting, or cross-domain condition is thinly studied.

## Decompose Novelty

A serious idea may be novel because it offers a new problem definition, perspective, mechanism, method, setting, evaluation lens, or boundary condition. Label the dominant novelty source explicitly.

## Search Lenses

- Gap-driven: what important thing is uncovered or weakly evidenced?
- Anomaly-driven: what keeps happening that the current explanation does not fit?
- Bottleneck-driven: what single bottleneck limits useful progress?
- Assumption-reversal: which default field assumption may be wrong or too narrow?
- Cross-domain transfer: what neighboring field solves the same structural problem differently?
- Writing-to-think: what becomes obvious after forcing a one-page problem definition and claim sketch?

## Mechanism Questions

Every serious candidate should answer why it should work, where it should work, where it should fail, what mechanism changes, what the core hypothesis is, and what the strongest falsification experiment is.

## Failure Value

Prefer routes that teach something if they fail. Low-information failure routes should be filtered out early.

## Local-Optimum Lock-In Check

Before promotion, ask which hidden assumption about bottleneck, data, objective, evaluator, or system boundary is being treated as fixed. Keep at least one non-local or assumption-reversal alternative alive long enough to avoid promoting the easiest continuation by inertia.
