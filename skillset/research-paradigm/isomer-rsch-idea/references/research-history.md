# Research History Playbook

Use this reference when the idea stage needs broad, history-aware literature search rather than a shallow scan of recent papers. The goal is to move from a field map to a lineage view and then to the real unresolved breakpoints.

## Field Map

Before deep reading, map the main problem, major subproblems, representative paradigms, common datasets and metrics, recurring disputes, and failure modes.

For a broad topic, start with a few strong reviews or tutorials, recent representative papers, and seminal or foundational papers. Do not start with deep reading of one narrow recent cluster and mistake it for the field.

## Paper Roles

For any serious direction, identify at least one foundational paper, one turning-point paper, and current mainstream or strongest recent papers.

## Citation Chaining

Use backward and forward citation chaining to reconstruct how the problem was originally defined, which assumptions survived, which assumptions changed, and which assumptions were never seriously tested.

## Search Ladder

1. Direct neighborhood: same task, dataset, and metric.
2. Failure-mode neighborhood: same bottleneck, breakdown pattern, or contradiction.
3. Mechanism neighborhood: same core idea in the same or adjacent tasks.
4. Cross-domain neighborhood: structurally similar solutions from nearby fields.

## Review Intent

Use narrative review for viewpoint formation, scoping review for broad gap discovery, and systematic review for tighter evidence synthesis on a focused question. The idea stage usually starts with field mapping and scoping, then narrows to targeted comparison.

## Stop Condition

The history pass is good enough when you can answer what the field treats as the mainline, what its strongest assumptions are, where contradictions or blind spots remain, and why the current candidate matters despite strongest nearby work.
