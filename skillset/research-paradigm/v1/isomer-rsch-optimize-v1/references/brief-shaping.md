# Brief Shaping

Use this reference when a candidate direction is still fuzzy and needs to become a structured, ranking-ready method brief.

## Clarify Before Widening

Resolve the concrete bottleneck, evaluation or comparability boundary, main hard constraint, and incumbent that the brief must beat or complement. If one unknown would materially change every candidate, clarify it before generating a noisy slate.

## Small Differentiated Slate

Default target: two or three serious approaches. The slate should usually include one incumbent-deepening refinement, one orthogonal mechanism, and one broader shift candidate when justified.

For each candidate, write bottleneck, why current line is limited, mechanism, why now, keep-unchanged contract, expected gain, and main risks.

## Shared Comparison Surface

Compare candidates on expected upside, comparability safety, implementation surface, mechanism distinctness, failure risk, and why this route is better now than nearby alternatives.

## Lead Brief

Recommend exactly one lead brief unless the frontier explicitly justifies more than one. Explain why the lead is the best move now, why alternatives are deferred, and what evidence would quickly disconfirm it.

## Self-Check

Before promotion, ask whether the bottleneck is concrete, the limitation is real, why now is evidence-based, the comparability boundary is explicit, ranking is based on tradeoffs rather than convenience, and the brief would make sense without chat context.

## Output Shape

1. brief title
2. one-paragraph bottleneck and constraint summary
3. two or three candidate comparison rows
4. recommended brief with tradeoff summary
5. self-check outcome
6. fields ready for `references/method-brief.md`
