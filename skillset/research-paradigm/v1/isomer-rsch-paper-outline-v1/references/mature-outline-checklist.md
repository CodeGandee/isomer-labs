# Mature Outline Checklist

Use this checklist before handing a full empirical outline to writing.

## Required Paper View

- central thesis and central insight are reader-facing, not metric summaries.
- insight ladder shows how observed facts become allowed interpretations.
- one to three scoped claims each have evidence needed and falsification conditions.
- closest-neighbor or novelty boundary explains what the paper claims and does not claim against prior or obvious alternatives.
- at least three likely reviewer objections map to analysis, writing, claim downgrade, or accepted limitation.
- evaluation plan names setting, datasets or benchmarks, baselines, metrics, and controlled factors.

## Analysis Quantity

A mature empirical paper usually plans four to eight reviewer-facing analysis jobs beyond the headline result. The broader evidence package usually needs five to ten ready experiment or analysis groups before being treated as strong. If the scope is narrower, include an analysis-budget waiver with a real reason and downgrade maturity or claim scope.

## Process-Language Cleanliness

Paper-facing fields must not mention local process, operator instructions, route history, local branch names, record ids, exact file paths, raw execution shorthand, or internal staging language. Put reproducibility facts in evidence view or appendix-only fields.

## Evidence Integrity

Check for stale duplicate ids, unsupported claims, hidden evidence gaps, and claims whose failure conditions are unspecified. If the outline cannot say what would weaken a claim, the claim is not mature.

## Handoff

A mature outline should compile into section writing jobs: what each section must prove, which evidence it uses, which displays it needs, which citations it needs, and which appendix bridge it requires.
