# Paper Outline Seed

Use this reference when the selected route is strong enough that the next serious step will likely become a paper-facing line. The goal is to seed one lightweight structured outline candidate so later Runs and analyses are not free-floating.

## When to Seed

- The idea survived literature and feasibility checks.
- The likely contribution is clear enough to name one to three research questions.
- The first main experiment and at least one likely follow-up analysis family can be anticipated.

## Minimal Seed Fields

- working title
- central thesis
- central insight
- reader takeaway
- problem, gap, method, main result placeholder, and scope limit
- one to three Research Claims with evidence needed and falsification condition
- method abstraction with intuition and mechanism steps
- evaluation plan with datasets, baselines, metrics, and controlled factors
- analysis plan with claim links, target display, role, and failure interpretation
- evidence grounding with observed facts, allowed interpretations, must-not-claim list, and evidence gaps
- research questions, experimental designs, sections, and required Evidence Items

## When Not to Seed

Do not seed an outline when the task framing is unstable, the candidate frontier is too wide, or the main claim cannot yet be stated.
