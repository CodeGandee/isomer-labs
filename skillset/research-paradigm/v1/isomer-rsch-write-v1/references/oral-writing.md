# Oral Writing

Use this reference when narrative spine, section discipline, reader onboarding, or reviewer-facing tone is weak.

## Reader Cognition

Write so the reader understands the core idea quickly, remembers the main claim, trusts the evidence, and can repeat the contribution accurately. Compression is not the same as sophistication.

## Story Spine

Before polishing prose, define the problem, the deficiency in current methods, the core idea, why the idea is principled, the main empirical pattern, the key mechanism or explanation, and the exact scope of the claim. Local sentence polish cannot fix a weak spine.

## Section Jobs

`Abstract` compresses the whole paper into one coherent impression. `Introduction` motivates, frames the gap, previews the contribution, and makes the reader care. `Related Work` makes novelty legible. `Method` teaches and defends the method. `Experiments` establishes the main empirical pattern. `Analysis` explains why the pattern is credible and important. `Conclusion` restates contribution, scope, and implication without overselling.

## Paragraph Jobs

Each paragraph should usually do one main thing: set up a problem, state a pattern, interpret a display, answer an objection, or explain a mechanism. Split paragraphs that simultaneously motivate, report numbers, argue novelty, and calibrate claims.

## Results and Analysis Prose

Do not narrate numbers as a spreadsheet. Use a three-layer structure: state the main pattern, anchor it with one or two key facts, then interpret why it matters. For analysis, name the stable trend, tradeoff, mechanism, and scope boundary.

## Figures and Tables

Introduce every important display by saying why it appears and what question it answers. Close it by stating the takeaway and what the reader should remember. Let the display carry detailed values and let prose carry interpretation.

## Claim Calibration

Calibrate claims structurally through wording, comparison choice, evidence organization, limitations, and conclusion. Prefer scoped language such as "strong default", "wins or ties most settings", or "more robust under sweep" only when the evidence supports that exact form.

## Appendix and Reproducibility

Treat appendix as part of the support package. It should contain protocol detail, full result matrices, sensitivity checks, tuned baselines, failure cases, extended examples, proofs, or reproducibility detail with clear bridges from main text.
