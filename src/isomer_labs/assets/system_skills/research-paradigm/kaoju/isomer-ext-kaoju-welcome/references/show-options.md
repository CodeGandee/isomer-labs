# Show Kaoju Options

## Workflow

1. Present the curated patterns in **Typical Use Cases** before the complete command inventory.
2. Explain that discovery finds candidates, acquisition creates governed local access, and examination produces source-grounded evidence.
3. Distinguish genuine reproduction from a bounded method trial before recommending execution.
4. Label phrase examples as representative routing cues and identify any scope, source, template, execution, network, or publication Gate.
5. Preserve the survey goal, sources, evidence refs, code or dataset context, and requested output in the public invocation without executing it.

If the goal does not map cleanly to these patterns, use the native planning tool to compare the evidence stage, desired output, accepted refs, resource limits, and required interaction, then recommend one public command or ask for the smallest missing decision.

## Typical Use Cases

| Kaoju topic initialization | Use when a generic Research Topic and concrete overview exist but Kaoju derived mindset intent is missing, stale, or needs explicit repair. | "initialize Kaoju for this topic," "create the topic mindsets," "reconcile the mindset source" | One reconciled Research Topic, Topic Workspace, and concrete `topic.intent.overview`. | `create-topic` | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use create-topic to initialize Kaoju derived intent for the selected topic` | The entrypoint delegates any missing generic state to the core Topic Creator, then creates only missing topic-owned Mindset Sources. | Mutates only Kaoju derived intent after generic state is ready; welcome changes nothing and installation never scans or writes topics. | Inspect the Sources or start one concrete Kaoju research action. |
| Direction framing | Use when a broad topic needs a bounded Survey Contract, clarified scope, research directions, evidence depth, resources, and stop conditions. | "choose survey directions," "frame this topic," "define the survey scope" | Topic, intended audience, scope constraints, evidence depth, resources, and material user choices. | `choose-directions` | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use choose-directions to frame a survey of reliable retrieval-augmented generation` | The entrypoint clarifies material choices and records bounded directions and scope. | Mutates survey framing only after required user interaction; welcome changes nothing. | Review and select the directions before discovery. |
| Landscape discovery | Use when the field needs broad discovery, source-family mapping, evidence intake, audit, and synthesis. | "survey the landscape," "map the field," "find the major approaches" | Survey Contract or topic scope, source types, recency bounds, and search budget. | `landscape-pass` | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use landscape-pass to map recent evaluation methods for retrieval-augmented generation` | The pass frames, discovers, acquires, examines, audits, and synthesizes a bounded landscape. | May use networks and create durable survey records under source-selection and other Gates. | Review coverage and add focused intake or comparison. |
| Reading-list construction | Use when a bounded set of papers, reports, repositories, datasets, or models must be discovered before local intake. | "build a reading list," "find key papers," "expand these seed sources" | Topic and scope, seed sources, source kinds, date or venue limits, and selection criteria. | `build-reading-list` | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use build-reading-list to find foundational and recent sources for the supplied survey` | The command discovers candidates and preserves query and selection provenance. | Discovery may use networks; acquisition is a separate governed action. | Select sources, then ingest accepted items. |
| Reading-item intake | Use when one selected paper or report must be acquired, examined, and linked into the evidence ledger. | "ingest this paper," "read and record this report" | Stable source identity or locator, access constraints, survey context, and expected evidence questions. | `ingest-reading-item` | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use ingest-reading-item to acquire and examine the supplied paper` | The command acquires permitted content, verifies identity, examines full text, and records source-grounded evidence. | May use networks and store source material only after source and access boundaries are satisfied. | Audit the new evidence or ingest the next selected source. |
| Curated evidence intake | Use when a known source set should be resolved, acquired, examined, and audited without broad landscape discovery. | "ingest these references," "process this curated source list" | Curated source list, expected identities, survey scope, access constraints, and audit target. | `curated-intake-pass` | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use curated-intake-pass to ingest and audit the supplied references` | The pass preserves source identity and provenance through accepted evidence. | May acquire and record sources under access and network Gates. | Inspect audit gaps or proceed to comparison and synthesis. |
| Theory comparison | Use when named works or methods need source-grounded conceptual comparison and audited synthesis. | "compare these theories," "contrast the assumptions," "build a claim table" | Named accepted sources, comparison dimensions, survey scope, and required evidence locators. | `theory-comparison-pass` | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use theory-comparison-pass to compare the supplied methods by assumptions and evidence` | The pass examines evidence, compares claims, audits traceability, and synthesizes supported conclusions. | Mutates comparison and synthesis records; it must not infer unsupported equivalence. | Review uncertainties and decide whether empirical comparison is needed. |
| Comparative evidence | Use when methods or sources require a controlled comparison contract, actual bounded runs, fairness limits, and audit. | "compare these implementations," "run a fair comparison" | Accepted sources or code, comparison dimensions, datasets, metrics, resource budget, and execution approval. | `comparative-pass` | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use comparative-pass to compare the two supplied implementations under one bounded evaluation contract` | The pass frames, trials, compares, and audits the approved comparison. | Executes code only after preparation and execution Gates; later actions are not preapproved. | Review the audit and synthesize supported differences. |
| Source-code intake | Use when a selected repository must be acquired, identity-verified, examined, and registered before a trial or comparison. | "ingest this repository," "inspect the source code," "map paper to code" | Repository locator, immutable revision, license and access posture, related source refs, and survey questions. | `ingest-source-code` | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use ingest-source-code to acquire and examine the supplied repository at the selected revision` | The command governs checkout, verifies source identity, inspects code, and records source evidence. | Uses networks and writes local source state only after source selection and access boundaries. | Prepare an environment or compare the examined source. |
| Code environment preparation | Use when accepted source code needs a governed environment and task-critical smoke check before any bounded run. | "prepare the code environment," "make this repository runnable" | Registered source revision, platform, package constraints, resource limits, and intended trial. | `prepare-code-run` | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use prepare-code-run to prepare the registered repository for one bounded trial` | The command routes operational setup through managed support and records readiness or blockers. | Mutates environment state; it does not authorize the later trial. | Inspect readiness, then separately approve the bounded trial. |
| Bounded code trial | Use when prepared source code should run one approved task-critical trial without claiming full paper reproduction. | "run a smoke trial," "test this method once," "execute the bounded probe" | Prepared environment, registered source, trial contract, resource budget, inputs, metrics, and explicit execution approval. | `run-code-trial` | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use run-code-trial to execute the approved smoke trial for the registered repository` | The command runs one bounded trial, records observations, limitations, and terminal status. | Executes code under a separate Gate; the example does not grant that approval. | Audit results and decide whether comparison or further trial is justified. |
| Genuine reproduction | Use when the user explicitly claims or requests faithful reproduction of a paper result rather than an ordinary method trial. | "reproduce this paper," "verify the published result" | Paper, source identity, fidelity criteria, data, metrics, environment, resource budget, and reproduction claim boundary. | Task-only entrypoint | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint evaluate whether the supplied paper and code are ready for a genuine reproduction contract` | The entrypoint selects the reproduction workflow only when fidelity requirements are explicit. | May require acquisition and substantial execution under dedicated Gates; no reproduction claim is assumed. | Approve the fidelity contract or choose a bounded trial instead. |
| Survey audit and synthesis | Use when evidence, comparisons, trials, or draft conclusions need coverage and traceability audit before accepted synthesis. | "audit the survey," "synthesize accepted evidence," "check coverage gaps" | Accepted evidence refs, source ledger, comparison or trial records, scope contract, and desired conclusion form. | `audit-survey-pass` or task-only | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use audit-survey-pass to audit the accepted evidence and synthesize only supported conclusions` | The entrypoint audits provenance, coverage, fairness, and traceability, then synthesizes only if accepted. | Mutates audit and synthesis records but does not acquire or execute by default. | Resolve gaps or proceed to paper or wiki output. |
| Paper production | Use when accepted audit and synthesis are ready for canonical paper drafting, template management, and PDF construction. | "draft the survey paper," "manage the paper template," "build the PDF" | Accepted audit and synthesis refs, target structure, content or LaTeX template role, publication constraints, and output goal. | `draft-paper`, `manage-paper-template`, or `build-paper-pdf` | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use draft-paper to write a survey paper from the accepted audit and synthesis` | The selected command writes canonical paper content, manages the chosen template role, or derives and validates a PDF. | Mutates paper or template state; TeX and PDF remain derived outputs, and publication needs its own Gate. | Review the paper, manage templates if needed, then build or revise. |
| Wiki export | Use when accepted survey records should become a self-contained LLM Wiki or governed local viewer. | "export the survey wiki," "build a self-contained knowledge base" | Accepted survey and source refs, export location, viewer needs, and any exposure decision. | `export-survey-wiki` | `$isomer-ext-kaoju-entrypoint use export-survey-wiki to export the accepted survey records as a self-contained wiki` | The command renders accepted records into a portable wiki and optional governed viewer package. | Writes export files; viewer launch or exposure requires a separate Gate. | Inspect the export and decide separately whether to launch or publish it. |

Discovery does not acquire, acquisition does not prove examination, and examination does not prove audit acceptance. A bounded trial does not justify a reproduction claim.
