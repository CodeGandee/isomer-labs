# Show DeepSci Options

## Workflow

1. Present the curated patterns in **Typical Use Cases** before the complete pass inventory.
2. Label phrase examples as representative routing cues, not required keywords.
3. If one pattern is selected, preserve the research question, hypothesis, evidence, manuscript, reviewer feedback, and readiness context in the public invocation.
4. Explain prerequisites, expected mutation, and the likely next decision or Gate without executing the route.
5. Use task-only entrypoint requests for focused scouting, baseline, experiment, analysis, decision, writing, plotting, or science-review work when no named pass is a better fit.

If the goal does not map cleanly to these patterns, use the native planning tool to compare the research stage, available evidence, intended output, and readiness, then recommend one public pass or task-only entrypoint request.

## Typical Use Cases

| Use Case | When to Use | Representative Routing Cues | Required Context | Canonical Route | Exact Invocation | Expected Action | Mutation Posture | Next Step |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hypothesis development and test | Use when a framed research question should become or evaluate one falsifiable hypothesis through bounded evidence. | "test this hypothesis," "turn this idea into an empirical claim" | Research question, current hypothesis or idea, Topic context, comparator basis, and evaluation intent. | `hypothesis-pass` | `$isomer-ext-deepsci-entrypoint use hypothesis-pass to test whether the supplied method improves robustness under distribution shift` | The entrypoint runs the selected hypothesis through its accepted experimental and analysis handoffs. | May create experiments and durable research records under DeepSci contracts; welcome changes nothing. | Verify readiness, then invoke the pass. |
| Broad empirical loop | Use when framing, baseline, candidate work, experiments, analysis, and decision should run as one bounded production pass. | "run an empirical pass," "evaluate this route end to end" | Research goal, Topic and workspace readiness, metrics, resource budget, and checkpoint preferences. | `empirical-pass` | `$isomer-ext-deepsci-entrypoint use empirical-pass to evaluate the supplied graph-learning route against the accepted baselines` | The entrypoint coordinates one linear empirical recipe and returns a verified terminal report. | Mutates experiment and durable evidence state; later Gates and blockers remain active. | Supply the evaluation contract and invoke. |
| Focused scouting or idea work | Use when the inquiry lacks literature orientation, benchmark framing, comparator direction, or a falsifiable research route. | "scout the field," "generate a research idea," "clarify the benchmark" | Research question, known constraints, available sources, and desired decision. | Task-only entrypoint | `$isomer-ext-deepsci-entrypoint scout the supplied question and recommend a falsifiable route without running experiments` | The entrypoint selects the focused framing capability and returns evidence or a blocker. | May write accepted research records if requested; no experiment is implied. | Review the proposed route, then choose a pass or focused next task. |
| Baseline and experiment preparation | Use when a trustworthy comparator or evaluation contract is missing before measured work can start. | "establish a baseline," "prepare the experiment," "define metrics" | Selected hypothesis, datasets, metrics, comparator candidates, environment, and resource limits. | Task-only entrypoint | `$isomer-ext-deepsci-entrypoint establish a trustworthy baseline and experiment contract for the supplied hypothesis` | The entrypoint selects bounded baseline or experiment preparation and reports readiness. | May change code, environment, or durable experiment state only through the selected owner. | Resolve any baseline waiver or blocker before running. |
| Experiment and analysis iteration | Use when a selected route is ready for one measured run or existing results need ablations, robustness, failure, or limitation analysis. | "run this experiment," "analyze failures," "add an ablation" | Accepted hypothesis or route, comparator, evaluation contract, environment, and existing result refs if any. | Task-only entrypoint | `$isomer-ext-deepsci-entrypoint run one bounded experiment for the accepted route and analyze the resulting failure modes` | The entrypoint performs the focused measured work and evidence-backed analysis. | Executes code and records evidence under resource and output policies; welcome does not run it. | Review evidence and make an explicit route decision. |
| Evidence-backed decision | Use when accumulated evidence requires a continue, stop, branch, reset, write, or finalize choice. | "decide whether to continue," "select the next route," "is this ready to write" | Accepted evidence refs, alternatives, limitations, resource state, and user-sensitive choices. | Task-only entrypoint | `$isomer-ext-deepsci-entrypoint decide whether the accepted evidence supports writing or another experiment` | The entrypoint records a justified decision and terminal refs. | Mutates durable decision state; user-sensitive choices may pause for input. | Follow only the recorded next route. |
| Paper development | Use when accepted evidence is ready to become an outlined, drafted, plotted, and reviewed paper bundle. | "draft the paper," "turn findings into a manuscript," "make the figures" | Accepted findings, claim boundaries, paper goal, target venue if known, and current manuscript state. | `paper-pass` | `$isomer-ext-deepsci-entrypoint use paper-pass to turn the accepted findings into a reviewed paper bundle` | The pass develops the paper from evidence through writing, figures, and review. | Mutates manuscript and paper artifacts; it must not invent unsupported claims. | Inspect the reviewed bundle and choose revision or polish. |
| Self-review and revision | Use when a substantial draft needs skeptical review, evidence-gap repair, and bounded revision. | "review and revise this draft," "fill the evidence gaps" | Draft bundle, accepted evidence, claim map, and revision scope. | `revision-pass` | `$isomer-ext-deepsci-entrypoint use revision-pass to review the current draft and revise supported gaps` | The pass audits the draft, routes bounded evidence work, and revises accepted text. | Mutates manuscript and possibly evidence state; unresolved gaps remain explicit. | Review remaining blockers or move to polish. |
| Reviewer rebuttal | Use when formal reviewer feedback must become evidence work, manuscript deltas, and a durable response. | "prepare the rebuttal," "address reviewer two" | Reviewer comments, current manuscript, accepted evidence, deadline, and permitted experiment budget. | `rebuttal-pass` | `$isomer-ext-deepsci-entrypoint use rebuttal-pass to address the supplied reviewer feedback with bounded evidence` | The pass maps comments to responses, evidence, experiments, limitations, and revised text. | May run bounded work and mutate rebuttal and manuscript artifacts under Gates. | Review unresolved comments and final response wording. |
| Polish and finalize | Use when figures and prose are already meaningful and need presentation-quality refinement before external review. | "polish the figures," "improve the prose," "finalize the bundle" | Reviewed manuscript, accepted figures and data, venue constraints, and outstanding limitations. | `polish-pass` | `$isomer-ext-deepsci-entrypoint use polish-pass to polish the reviewed manuscript and figures` | The pass refines presentation without changing unsupported scientific meaning. | Mutates presentation artifacts and prose; it must preserve evidence boundaries. | Run submission preparation when the bundle is accepted. |
| Submission or archive | Use when a reviewed paper bundle is ready for final checks, submission packaging, or governed archival handoff. | "prepare for submission," "finalize the archive" | Reviewed bundle, target venue or archive, data and code availability plan, and publication decisions. | `submission-pass` | `$isomer-ext-deepsci-entrypoint use submission-pass to prepare the reviewed bundle for the selected venue` | The pass finalizes required artifacts and returns submission or archive evidence. | May package and expose outputs only under applicable publication or network Gates. | Complete the external submission or retain the archive evidence. |
| Readiness recovery | Use when the desired DeepSci task lacks Project, Topic Workspace, actor or agent workspace, environment, extension, or bootstrap readiness. | "DeepSci is unavailable," "workspace is not ready," "missing environment" | Agent host and scope, Project and Topic, intended actor or agent, and the concrete research target. | Core or DeepSci entrypoint | `$isomer-op-entrypoint use system-skills to inspect the DeepSci pack for this host and scope` | The public owners inspect or recover extension and workspace prerequisites before research begins. | Inspection is read-only; installation, workspace, and environment recovery mutate only when requested. | Resolve the reported prerequisite, refresh the host if needed, then resume the research target. |

The named pass commands remain bounded single-pass procedures. Task-only requests select focused research work without exposing private capability identities.
